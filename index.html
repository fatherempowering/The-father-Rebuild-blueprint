The Framework

4 Rebuild
Principles

01
Protein Anchors Every Meal

Not sometimes. Not when convenient. Every meal has a protein anchor. Without this, recovery fails, muscle breaks down, and hunger drives poor decisions.

02
Eat for Recovery, Not Energy

Fathers over 38 are in a constant recovery deficit. The goal is not to fuel workouts. The goal is to fuel function, work, training, family, and sleep.

03
Reduce Decision Load at Night

Your worst nutritional decisions happen after 7PM. Eliminate the decision. Structure your environment so the right choice requires no effort.

04
Consistency Beats Precision

An 80% plan followed every day destroys a perfect plan followed twice a week. Reduce friction, increase adherence. Build the habit first.

Rebuild Standard · 01

Protein

PROTEIN

Daily Target

  • 0.8 – 1g per lb of bodyweight
  • Spread across 3–4 meals
  • Minimum 35–45g per meal
  • Non-negotiable on rest days

Best Sources

  • Chicken breast / thighs
  • Ground beef (85/15)
  • Eggs + egg whites
  • Greek yogurt
  • Cottage cheese
  • Whey protein (backup only)
Field Rule
Protein first. Every meal.
Non-negotiable.

Rebuild Standard · 02

Carbohydrates

CARBS

Timing Rules

  • Priority: pre/post training window
  • Reduce on rest days
  • Avoid high-carb meals late evening
  • Focus on whole food sources

Best Sources

  • White rice (easy to digest)
  • Oats (morning anchor)
  • Sweet potato
  • Fruit (post-training)
  • Sourdough / whole grain bread
Field Rule
Earn your carbs with your training.
Time them around your effort.
Rebuild Standard · 03

Fats & Recovery

FATS

Why It Matters

  • Supports testosterone production
  • Regulates inflammation
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Reduces cortisol response

Best Sources

  • Whole eggs
  • Olive oil
  • Avocado
  • Fatty fish (salmon, sardines)
  • Mixed nuts (controlled portions)
Field Rule
Don't fear fat. Fear processed food
disguised as healthy fuel.
